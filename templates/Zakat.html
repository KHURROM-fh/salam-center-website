Zakat

إِنَّمَا الصَّدَقَاتُ لِلْفُقَرَاءِ وَالْمَسَاكِينِ وَالْعَامِلِينَ عَلَيْهَا وَالْمُؤَلَّفَةِ قُلُوبُهُمْ وَفِي الرِّقَابِ وَالْغَارِمِينَ وَفِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَابْنِ السَّبِيلِ فَرِيضَةً مِنَ اللَّهِ وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ

Indeed, [prescribed] charitable offerings are only [to be given] to the poor and the indigent, and to those who work on [administering] it, and to those whose hearts are to be reconciled, and to [free] those in bondage, and to the debt-ridden, and for the cause of God, and to the wayfarer. [This is] an obligation from God. And God is all-knowing, all-wise. ~ [Al-Tawbah, 9:60]

Integrity and Transparency in
Zakat Distribution

Here is a summary of how we handle the Zakat funds:

  • Collection: We collect Zakat from our community through various channels such as online payments, bank transfers, and in-person donations. We maintain a separate account exclusively for Zakat funds to ensure there is no mixing with other types of funds.
  • Verification: We verify the eligibility of Zakat recipients by assessing their needs and financial situations. This includes reviewing financial statements and ensuring that funds are distributed to the most vulnerable members of our community.
  • Distribution: We distribute Zakat funds to eligible recipients in a timely and efficient manner. Our goal is to provide immediate assistance to those in need, including food, shelter, and educational support.
  • Record Keeping: We maintain detailed records of all Zakat transactions for audit purposes. These records include the source of funds, distribution details, and recipient information.
  • Reporting: We prepare monthly and annual reports for the SLAVAI Board of Trustees, which include details of Zakat funds collected, distributed, and remaining. These reports are made available to the public to ensure transparency.

The Eight Categories of Eligible Zakat Recipients

  1. The Poor (Al-Fuqara): Those who lack basic necessities.
  2. The Needy (Al-Masakin): Those who are in difficult financial situations.
  3. Zakat Workers: Those who are involved in the collection and distribution of Zakat.
  4. Those Whose Hearts Are to Be Reconciled: New Muslims or those who may be inclined towards Islam.
  5. Freeing Slaves: Assisting in the liberation of individuals from bondage.
  6. Those in Debt: Individuals who are burdened by debt for legitimate purposes.
  7. In the Cause of Allah: Supporting projects or initiatives that benefit the Muslim community.
  8. Wayfarers: Travelers who are stranded and in need of assistance.

Zakat Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Who is eligible to receive Zakat money?

    Eligibility for Zakat is determined based on specific criteria outlined in Islamic law. Generally, those in financial need, the poor, and the needy are eligible.

  • How can I apply to receive Zakat money?

    You can apply for Zakat assistance by visiting our office or submitting an online application through our website.

  • What documentation do I need to provide to receive Zakat money?

    You will need to provide proof of identity, proof of income, and any other relevant documents that demonstrate your financial need.

  • What is the Zakat review process?

    The Zakat review process involves verifying your application and supporting documents to ensure eligibility. This process typically takes a few business days.

  • How is the amount of Zakat money I receive determined?

    The amount of Zakat you receive is determined based on your financial situation, the funds available, and the number of applicants.

  • Can I apply for Zakat for my family?

    Yes, you can apply for Zakat on behalf of your family members if they meet the eligibility criteria.

  • Can I apply for Zakat online?

    Yes, we offer an online application process for your convenience. Visit our website to apply.

  • How long after I submit my application can I expect a response?

    You can expect a response within 5-7 business days after submitting your application.

  • Is there anything I can do to appeal for more money if I receive less than I need?

    Yes, you can submit an appeal with additional documentation to support your request for more assistance.

  • Can I receive Zakat money more than once?

    Yes, you can apply for Zakat assistance multiple times if you continue to meet the eligibility criteria.

Not sure if you are eligible? Consult this FAQ or ask SALAM office.

How can I apply to receive Zakat money?

To apply for Zakat at SALAM, you must complete and submit to the SALAM office the Zakat application obtained from the office, with all supporting documents.

Submit your completed application with the required documentation in person at the SALAM Center Office 4545 College Oak Drive. If an application is submitted without the required documents, it is considered incomplete and will be rejected.

What documentation do I need to provide to receive Zakat money?

You must submit a copy of your picture ID and all those in your household, proof of your income, expenses (Rent statement, lease etc), and a bank statement/s showing the last three months.
